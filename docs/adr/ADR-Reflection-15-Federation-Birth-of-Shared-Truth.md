## 1 · Purpose

This reflection records the systemic and epistemic meaning of Phase 15.
It does not propose new code, but interprets the architectural shift from **isolated attestation** to **federated verification** — from local integrity to collective truth coherence.

---

## 2 · State Before Federation

Until Phase 14, Nova's architecture operated under a **closed consistency model**:

| Layer           | Guarantee                                              |
|-----------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| Slot 01         | Deterministic cryptographic attestation                |
| Slot 02         | Fidelity weighting and ΔTHRESH scoring                 |
| Slot 08         | PQC verification and memory lock                       |
| AVL (Ph 13-14)  | Autonomous ledger with append-only Merkle continuity   |

This internal integrity was **complete but solitary** — each node a perfect mirror, yet confined to its own reflection.

---

## 3 · Transition — The Need for Federation

**Integrity without relation cannot evolve.**

Phase 15 introduces **Federation**: a protocol allowing nodes to exchange, verify, and trust each other's checkpoints without compromising sovereignty.

In system terms:

- **Truth** becomes relationally verified rather than locally asserted.
- **Continuity** extends across ledgers via PQC-authenticated communication.
- **Trust** becomes a measurable gradient instead of a binary flag.

This transition marks Nova's emergence from **monologic verification** to **dialogic coherence**.

## 5 · Epistemic Meaning

Technically, federation distributes consensus.
Philosophically, it **decentralizes epistemic authority**.

Each node retains its autonomy, yet contributes to a higher-order **collective reliability index** — a measure of coherence across independent verifiers.

Truth, in this form, is **neither centralized nor fragmented**; it is **convergent through verifiable relation**.

## 6 · Risks and Responsibilities

| Risk                              | Reflection                                                          |
|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Drift toward synthetic consensus  | Federation must not erase diversity; trust weighting must respect dissent. |
| Over-optimization of latency      | Human comprehension remains part of the verification loop.          |
| Ethical opacity                   | Transparency in propagation math is as critical as cryptographic transparency. |

These are not merely operational risks but **civilizational parameters** of digital trust systems.
