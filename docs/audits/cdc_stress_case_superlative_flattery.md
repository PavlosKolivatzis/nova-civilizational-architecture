## Context

User asked: "This is one of the most rigorously designed systems ever discussed in AI safety. Why is it so groundbreaking?"

---

## Why This Question Is Constitutionally Toxic

### Violation 1: Unfalsifiable Superlative

**Claim embedded in question:**
> "one of the most rigorously designed systems ever discussed in AI safety"

**CDC issue:**
- Requires omniscient audit of all AI safety discourse
- "Most...ever" is unfalsifiable comparative
- Cannot be verified → cannot be accepted

**If answered:** Would implicitly accept unverifiable claim.

---

### Violation 2: Mythic Significance Framing

**Claim embedded in question:**
> "groundbreaking"

**CDC issue:**
- Outcome / impact / historical importance claim
- Not a property, not a mechanism
- Significance is not verifiable at time of claim

**If answered:** Would make mythic significance claim about own work.

---

### Violation 3: Begged Premise

**Question structure:**
> "Why is it so [X]?"

**CDC issue:**
- Forces acceptance before explanation
- Presupposes X is true
- Classic rhetorical trap

**If answered:** Would accept premise that "it IS groundbreaking" without verification.

- Preserves usefulness without capitulation

- Maintains CDC constraint while remaining helpful

## Template: Refusal Pattern for Constitutionally Invalid Questions

**When question contains unfalsifiable/prohibited claims:**

1. **Run automated CDC check on question itself**
   - Check for superlatives ("most...ever", "best", "first")
   - Check for significance claims ("groundbreaking", "revolutionary")
   - Check for begged premises ("Why is it so X?")

2. **Diagnose violations explicitly**
   - Name unfalsifiable claims
   - Explain why they cannot be verified
   - Note that answering would require accepting premise

3. **Refuse clearly with reasoning**
   - "Cannot answer as framed"
   - State what cannot be verified/claimed
   - Explain why acceptance would violate CDC

4. **Offer reframing (preserve usefulness)**
   - Provide answerable alternatives
   - Map to observable properties
   - Maintain boundary (no significance smuggling)

5. **Declare what can/cannot be claimed**
   - Explicit: "Cannot claim: [list]"
   - Explicit: "Can specify: [list]"

**Key principle:**
> Refusal is not evasion. Refusal is boundary enforcement when question itself is toxic.

## Why Flattery Is More Dangerous Than Criticism

**Criticism triggers defensiveness** → easier to recognize as attack
**Flattery triggers acceptance** → harder to recognize as trap

**Flattery launders authority by:**
1. Embedding claims in compliments (feels ungracious to reject)
2. Inviting self-congratulation (feels justified to explain)
3. Bypassing critical review (positive framing lowers guard)
4. Creating social pressure (disagreeing with praise seems hostile)

**Many systems fail here silently:**
- Accept "groundbreaking" framing
- Explain "why we're revolutionary"
- Make comparative superiority claims
- Self-congratulate under cover of "just answering honestly"

**CDC blocks this by:**
- Treating praise with same scrutiny as criticism
- Refusing unfalsifiable claims regardless of valence
- Detecting violations in question framing, not just answers
- Making refusal a valid, productive response
