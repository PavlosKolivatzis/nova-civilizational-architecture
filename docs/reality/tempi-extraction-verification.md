#### Legislative/Regulatory Control

**Claim**: "90% of Greek legislation is EU directives"

**Verified facts**:
- EU directives **must** be transposed into national law by member states
- Greece transposes via laws, presidential decrees, ministerial decisions
- EU target: <1% transposition deficit (meaning ~99% compliance expected)
- Specific Greece percentage: **Not found in search** (would need Single Market Scoreboard)

**Assessment**: ⚠️ **MECHANISM VERIFIED, PERCENTAGE UNVERIFIED**

**What's known**:
- EU directives are legally binding
- Member states must transpose them
- Greece has no option to refuse (only implementation method)
- Scope: Single market, environment, consumer protection, much regulation

**What's unknown**:
- Exact % of Greek laws originating from EU directives
- Distinction between mandatory transposition vs. independent Greek legislation
- Current Greece-specific transposition rate

**Reasonable estimate**: 60-80% of **regulatory/economic** legislation likely EU-derived (standard for EU members), not necessarily 90% of **all** legislation.

**Rollback needed**: "90%" is **unverified**; replace with "majority of regulatory legislation EU-sourced (60-80% estimate for typical EU member)"
